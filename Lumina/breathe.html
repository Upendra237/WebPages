Box Breathing

Used by Navy SEALs and first responders. Equal intervals create a steady rhythm that calms the mind and sharpens focus.

How It Works

Inhale — Fill your lungs slowly and steadily through your nose.

Hold — Pause at the top. Let your body absorb the oxygen.

Exhale — Release slowly through your mouth. Let go of tension.

Hold — Pause at the bottom. Rest in the stillness.

The Science

Extended exhales activate the vagus nerve — the main highway of your parasympathetic (calming) nervous system.

This reduces cortisol, lowers heart rate, relaxes muscles, and shifts your brain from reactive to responsive mode.

Research shows just 5 minutes of intentional breathing measurably reduces anxiety and improves focus.
